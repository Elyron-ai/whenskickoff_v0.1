# 02 · Users & market

## 1. Market focus: UK core + expat hubs

Decision (locked): launch UK-first, then expand to **expat hubs** — cities with large displaced-fan populations where both of the product's jobs hurt most: NYC, Dubai, Singapore, Sydney.

Why this shape:

- **UK** is where the designs, tone, TV landscape, and Favored's venue coverage already point; pub sport culture makes "where's it on" a weekly, mainstream question rather than a niche one.
- **Expat hubs** maximize *both* pains at once: kickoff is at 3am local *and* you don't have the local subscription, so a bar showing the match is often the only option. Nobody serves "Arsenal fan in Singapore" well.

Order-of-magnitude sizing (estimates, to be validated — treat as directional):

| Segment | Rough size | Note |
|---|---|---|
| UK adults who watch live sport in pubs at least monthly | ~8–10M | Pub-sport is a mainstream UK behaviour; Fanzo's existence and pub-side adoption proves venues pay for this demand |
| UK fans following a "wrong-timezone" sport (NFL, cricket tours, F1 flyaways) | ~3–5M | NFL UK fandom alone is ~millions and growing; every Ashes/India tour is a night-shift |
| British expats worldwide | ~5M | Concentrated exactly in the proposed hubs |
| Premier League followers in SG/UAE/AU/US metros | tens of millions | We only need the "watches out / needs the time localized" slice |

## 2. Personas & jobs-to-be-done

### Priya — the displaced fan (Singapore, Arsenal)
- **Trigger:** fixture list drops, or matchday morning.
- **Job:** *"Tell me when Arsenal kick off __my time__, and which bar near me shows it — at 3am if that's what it takes."*
- **Current workaround:** Google the UK time, do the maths, WhatsApp the supporters' club group chat for a venue.
- **We replace that with:** followed team + localized time + reminder + Nearby filtered to the fixture. The timezone maths *is* the onboarding promise ("We'll do the timezone maths.").

### Danny — the night-owl NFL fan (Leeds, Chiefs)
- **Trigger:** Sunday/Monday night games at 01:20 UK time.
- **Job:** *"Warn me it's a late one, and tell me if anywhere near me has a late licence — or which channel it's on so I can decide sofa vs. out."*
- **Current workaround:** NFL app (US-centric times), Sky EPG scrolling, guessing which pub stays open.
- **We replace that with:** the orange late-night nudge in the designs ("🦉 Late one tonight. Kickoff 01:20 your time — 2 pubs near you have a late licence.") — this persona is why that card exists.

### Sam — the group organiser (London)
- **Trigger:** a big match + a group chat ("final on Sunday — where are we watching?").
- **Job:** *"Find somewhere with a big screen, sound on, space for 8, food — and let me book it."*
- **Current workaround:** calling pubs; arriving 2 hours early to hold a table.
- **We replace that with:** Favored venue detail — screens/sound/room notes, amenities, and **Book table**. This is the persona with direct revenue attached (booking referrals, venue promotion).

### The multi-sport household (light fourth persona)
Football + cricket + F1 + occasional NFL in one home; today that's four apps and a TV guide. One Home feed of "today's kickoffs" with sport filters is the whole pitch. Low intensity, high breadth — this persona is why *multi-sport* beats a football-only clone.

## 3. Competitive landscape (UK)

| | Fixtures | TV listings | Venue discovery | Multi-sport | Timezone-native | Venue quality/editorial | Model |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fanzo** (ex-MatchPint) | partial | ✔ | ✔ **incumbent** | ✔ | ✘ | coverage-first, thin on quality | venue B2B |
| **wheresthematch.com** | ✔ | ✔ strong | ✘ | ✔ | ✘ | — | ads |
| **LiveFootballOnTV** | ✔ | ✔ | ✘ | football only | ✘ | — | ads |
| **FotMob / OneFootball / LiveScore** | ✔ deep | partial | ✘ | football-centric | partial (device time) | — | ads/affiliate |
| **Google / EPG apps** | ✔ | ✔ | ✘ | ✔ | ✔-ish | — | — |
| **WhensKickoff** | ✔ | ✔ curated | ✔ via Favored | ✔ | ✔ **core competence** | ✔ **Favored ratings + editorial** | affiliate + venue B2B + premium |

**Differentiation thesis.** WhensKickoff is the only product combining **multi-sport fixtures + timezone-native rendering + a quality-curated venue layer**. Fanzo does venues but coverage-first (every pub that ticks a box) with no serious quality signal; WhensKickoff inherits Favored's ratings, review editorial, and booking — a *taste* layer, not just a listings layer. TV-listings sites do channels but are desktop-era ad pages with no alerts, no personalization, no venues. Score apps do fixtures deeply but one sport at a time and without the "where to watch out" job at all.

**Where we lose (honestly).**
- Score depth: FotMob wins any user whose primary job is following the match minute-by-minute. We link out rather than compete.
- Day-one venue coverage: Fanzo has years of pub relationships; we counter with quality-over-coverage in a London pilot, not a national land-grab.
- Habit: Google is the zero-effort default for "when do X play"; our answer is the bell (you don't Google what pings you) and SEO pages that intercept those very queries.

## 4. Positioning & tone

**One-liner:** *"Never miss kickoff. Know where to watch."*

**Elevator:** WhensKickoff tells you when your teams play — in your timezone, across every sport you follow — and where to watch: the channel it's on, or a great pub nearby that's showing it, rated and bookable via Favored.

**Tone is a brand asset.** The designs already speak fluent fan: "Right, let's go →", "Your lot. We'll nudge you before every kickoff.", "one for the night owls 🦉", "We'll do the timezone maths." Codify it: warm, British, matey but competent; never corporate-sports ("premium content experience") and never laddish. The voice should read like the mate who always knows what's on and where.

**Brand-stretch note.** "Kickoff" is football-native; cricket has no kickoff and F1 has lights-out. Verdict: keep the name — "KO" flexes (boxing, NFL uses kickoff literally), the bubble-mark asks *"KO?"* like a text from a mate, and the tone carries the license ("kickoff" as shorthand for *start time* is universally understood). Revisit only if user research shows non-football fans bouncing off the name.
